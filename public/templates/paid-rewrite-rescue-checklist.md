# Paid-Rewrite Rescue Checklist

Work through the resume draft you paid for, section by section. Mark every line **KEEP** (genuinely better than your original), **FIX** (right idea, wrong words), or **MISSING** (something true about your work that isn't there at all). Twenty minutes, honestly done, tells you whether to push for another revision or rebuild.

## 1. The top of the page
- [ ] Does the summary describe YOU — or could it belong to anyone in your field?
- [ ] Does it point at the job you're going TOWARD (not the one you're leaving)?
- [ ] Is your target role's language in it — the words that side of the table uses?

## 2. Experience section — per role
- [ ] Are the tools and systems you actually used NAMED (not "various software/equipment")?
- [ ] Is required training on the page (safety, compliance, certifications you hold)?
- [ ] Does at least one bullet per role carry a real number — scale, volume, frequency, result?
- [ ] Did anything you're proud of get flattened into a duty list? Mark it FIX.
- [ ] Is anything on the page that you never said and isn't true? Mark it — that's a line you must remove, whoever wrote it.

## 3. Skills & credentials block
- [ ] Every license/cert you hold, with the real name a recruiter would search
- [ ] Nothing listed that you don't actually hold (a "planned" cert doesn't belong here)
- [ ] Trade/government/military terms translated where a civilian reader needs them

## 4. The dodge test
Count your KEEP lines against your FIX + MISSING lines.
- Mostly KEEP: the rewrite mostly worked — send one precise note per FIX line and finish it.
- Mostly FIX: one more round, with line-by-line notes (one change per note, tied to a specific line).
- Mostly MISSING: the service reformatted instead of rewriting. Stop chasing; rebuild from your real experience.
